CODING INSTRUCTIONS FOR CONSTRAINT VALIDATION SAMPLE
=====================================================

Thank you for participating in this validation exercise. Your task is to independently
classify each sentence in the validation sample into one of seven constraint categories,
or to mark it as unclassified.

READ THIS ENTIRE DOCUMENT BEFORE STARTING.


THE SEVEN CATEGORIES
--------------------

1. FINANCIAL
   Money, funding, capital, cost, credit, affordability, investment.
   Examples:
     "We had no money to build the first version."
     "The banks turned us down three times."
     "It was just too expensive to keep going."

2. MARKET
   Customer demand, competition, market size, adoption, sales traction.
   Examples:
     "Customers just didn't want the product."
     "There were a thousand other companies doing the same thing."
     "The market wasn't ready for what we had built."

3. INSTITUTIONAL
   Regulation, law, policy, compliance, licensing, government, bureaucracy.
   Examples:
     "The FDA approval process took eighteen months."
     "New regulations made our business model illegal overnight."
     "The licensing requirements were prohibitive."

4. CAPABILITY
   Lack of internal knowledge, expertise, technical skill, learning gaps.
   Examples:
     "We didn't know how to write software at all."
     "The learning curve was incredibly steep."
     "We had to teach ourselves everything from scratch."

5. TALENT
   Hiring, recruiting, retaining, or finding qualified people.
   Examples:
     "We couldn't find any experienced engineers."
     "There was a serious talent shortage in our field."
     "Everyone we hired left within six months."

6. NETWORK
   Industry connections, partnerships, mentors, ecosystem access.
   Examples:
     "We had zero connections when we started."
     "No one in the industry would take a meeting with us."
     "There was no ecosystem to plug into where we lived."

7. SELF_IMPOSED
   Voluntary limitations from values, ethics, mission, or principles.
   Examples:
     "We chose not to raise venture capital."
     "It was against our values to expand internationally."
     "We deliberately kept the company local."


UNCLASSIFIED
------------

Mark a sentence as "unclassified" if it does not clearly fit any of the seven
categories, or if it is not primarily about a constraint at all.


THREE IMPORTANT EXCLUSION RULES
-------------------------------

The following patterns often confuse coders. Please follow these rules strictly:

RULE 1: "Could not afford to hire X" is FINANCIAL, not TALENT.
        The binding constraint is money, not people.
        Example: "We couldn't afford to hire a senior developer" -> FINANCIAL

RULE 2: "Didn't know how to do X" is CAPABILITY, not TALENT.
        The binding constraint is internal knowledge, not recruitment.
        Example: "We didn't know how to build the machine learning stack" -> CAPABILITY

RULE 3: "For ethical/values reasons" is SELF_IMPOSED, regardless of surface topic.
        The binding constraint is voluntary, not externally imposed.
        Example: "We refused to work with defense contractors on principle" -> SELF_IMPOSED
                 (not INSTITUTIONAL, even though defense contracting is regulated)


HOW TO CODE
-----------

Open the file `validation_sample_CODER1.csv` (or CODER2, depending on your assignment)
in Excel, Numbers, LibreOffice, or Google Sheets. For each row:

1. Read the sentence in the `sentence_text` column.
2. Decide which single category best captures the constraint being described.
3. Enter the category name (lowercase, with underscore for self_imposed) in the
   `coder_label` column. Valid values:
     financial, market, institutional, capability, talent, network, self_imposed, unclassified

- DO NOT look at the `pipeline_label` column while coding. That is the classifier's
  own guess; your job is to code independently.
- DO NOT consult the other coder or discuss any sentences during coding.
- DO NOT search the web for the sentence or try to identify the episode.
